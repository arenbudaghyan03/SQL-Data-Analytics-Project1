This project explores sales, customer, and product data of a retail sales business using **SQL Server**.  
The goal is to understand business performance, customer demographics, and product distribution through structured queries and key metrics. It demonstrates my ability to **write production-quality analytical SQL, design efficient queries, and turn business questions into data-driven insights.** 

 **Executive Summary**
 
✅ **Project scope**: Built multiple queries that answer key questions about the business.

✅ **Data modeling**: Used multi-table joins across fact and dimension tables to extract insights.

✅ **Analytics**: Applied aggregations, filtering, and sorting to find top products by generated revenue

✅ **Outcomes**: Delivered actionable insights on potential growth strategy. Many of the outcomes might be used in the upcoming marketing campaigns to ensure high profitability and higher overall ROI.

🛠️ Tools I Used

## 🎯 The questions I wanted to answer through my SQL queries were:

1. What is our **geographic reach** and **customer base composition**?

2. What are our **key business performance metrics** (Sales, Orders, Quantity)?

3. Which product categories drive **the highest revenue?**

4. Who are our **most valuable customers** by lifetime revenue?

5. Which products are our **"star performers"** vs. **underperformers**?


📈 Dimension Analysis
----------------

This section focuses on exploring the **dimensions of the business data** (customers, products, and sales) providing a foundation for understanding the structure and key metrics.


📊 Magnitude Analysis
----------------

This section of the project focuses on understanding the **scale and distribution** of customers, products, and sales in the business. Each analysis provides insights into key business metrics.


⚖️ Ranking Analysis
----------------

This section focuses on **ranking products based on revenue**, helping to identify which products drive the most value and which contribute the least.

## 💡 Key Insights 

**High-Value Markets**: USA and Australia represent the largest customer segments, suggesting a focus on regional promotions there.

**Product Concentration**: Bikes generate the highest revenue, while having also the highest average cost per item.

**Customer Loyalty**: Identified a core group of "Power Users" who contribute disproportionately to total revenue.
